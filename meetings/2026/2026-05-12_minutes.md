### Formal Verification and Security Reviews

* Patrick Schaller: Formal models are useful, but the current state of the art still requires significant expertise about what is happening under the hood.

* Patrick Schaller: Results from formal verification are only valid within the model that was formalized. A simple model may be easy to prove, but the result is correspondingly limited.

* Patrick Schaller: More complex properties, such as zero-knowledge proof properties, require knowledge of the term algebra, the logic being used, and how to encode the model in a way that remains performant enough for the tool.

* Patrick Schaller: Formalizations can be brittle. They can become either too trivial to provide useful assurance, or too complex for the tool to handle effectively.

* Tommaso Innocenti: A useful goal may be to enable standards developers to create starter models that help them understand security properties, possible bugs, and assurance claims before release.

* Patrick Schaller: That goal is valuable, but in practice companies often still need support from experts who work on the formal verification tools in order to create the formalization and proofs.

* Simone Onofri: Formal verification may be an interesting topic for SING to investigate further, possibly through a small task force, to understand whether and how formal verification could be brought into W3C security review work.
